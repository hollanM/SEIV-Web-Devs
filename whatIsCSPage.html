WHAT IS COMPUTER SCIENCE?

COMPUTER SCIENCE IS THE STUDY OF HOW COMPUTERS WORK AND HOW WE USE THEM TO SOLVE REAL WORLD PROBLEMS. IT INVOLVES THINKING CRITICALLY, DESIGNING EFFECTIVE SOLUTIONS, AND DEVELOPING SOFTWARE THAT MEET REQUIREMENTS.

COMPUTER SCIENTISTS FOCUS ON BREAKING DOWN COMPLEX PROBLEMS INTO MANAGEABLE PARTS. THEY DESIGN TECHNOLOGICAL APPROACHES TO ADDRESS THESE CHALLENGES AND CREATE SOFTWARE THAT IMPROVES THE LIVES OF OTHERS.

THEY ALSO LEARN PROGRAMMING LANGUAGES, SOFTWARE ENGINEERING PRINCIPLES, COMPUTER ARCHITECTURE, AND DATABASE MANAGEMENT. COMPUTER SCIENCE ISN'T LIMITED TO A SINGLE FIELD, THERE IS A VARIETY TO CHOOSE FROM. ONCE FUNDAMENTALS PRINCIPLES ARE MASTERED, STUDENTS CAN EXPLORE AND SPECIALIZE IN VARIOUS AREAS, FROM ARTIFICIAL INTELLIGENCE TO GAME DEVELOPMENT AND MORE.
